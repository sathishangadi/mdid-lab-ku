Research Focus

The Metabolic Disorders & Infectious Diseases Research Lab (MDID Lab) explores a wide range of biological and biomedical challenges with a special focus on the intersection between metabolic dysfunctions and infectious diseases. Our multidisciplinary projects use both experimental and computational approaches to understand disease mechanisms and identify novel drug candidates.

ðŸ”¬ Major Research Areas

  • Anti-HIV Drug Discovery: Screening and identification of novel CCR5 antagonists, integrase inhibitors, and reverse transcriptase blockers through in silico molecular docking, pharmacophore modeling, and ADMET profiling.
  • Metabolic Disorders: Investigating bioactive compounds with anti-diabetic and anti-obesity properties using enzymatic inhibition assays and molecular simulations.
  • Computational Biology: Employing bioinformatics and cheminformatics tools for virtual screening, QSAR analysis, and molecular dynamics simulations to predict drug efficacy.
  • Phytochemistry & Ethnobotany: Identifying therapeutic compounds from medicinal plants traditionally used in Indian systems of medicine.
  • Antimicrobial Drug Resistance: Studying bacterial and fungal resistance mechanisms and evaluating new natural and synthetic inhibitors.
