About the Lab

The Metabolic Disorders & Infectious Diseases Research Laboratory (MDID Lab) at the Department of Zoology, Kakatiya University, focuses on integrative research addressing the dual challenges of metabolic syndromes and infectious diseases. Our multidisciplinary approach combines molecular biology, bioinformatics, pharmacology, and biotechnology to identify novel therapeutic agents from natural sources and computational methods.

About the Principal Investigator

Prof. Estari Mamidala, Ph.D., PDF (USA), ISZN, MRSB, is Professor of Zoology and Head of the MDID Research Lab at Kakatiya University, Telangana State, India. He obtained his Ph.D. in Zoology (2005) from Kakatiya University and completed his Post-Doctoral Fellowship (PDF) at the University of West Georgia and Emory University (USA) under the UGC–Raman Fellowship.

Prof. Mamidala has published over 130 research papers in reputed international journals and holds 10 patents. His group focuses on anti-HIV drug discovery, metabolic disorders, molecular docking, and pharmacognosy, guiding numerous Ph.D. and M.Phil. scholars in life sciences.

Collaborations & Contributions

Prof. Mamidala collaborates internationally with research groups in the USA, Netherlands, Malaysia, and Spain. His recent work includes COVID-19 drug design, CCR5-targeted HIV inhibitors, and α-glucosidase inhibition studies, featured in top journals like Nature Scientific Reports and Materials Today: Proceedings.
